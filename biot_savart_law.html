Biot-Savart Law

The Biot-Savart Law describes the magnetic field at a point in space due to a moving charge.

The magnetic field $\overrightarrow{B}$ at a point $\overrightarrow{r}$ due to a charge $q$ at point $\overrightarrow{r_q}$ moving with velocity $\overrightarrow{v}$ is $$ \overrightarrow{B}(\overrightarrow{r}) = {\mu_0 \over 4\pi} {\overrightarrow{v} \times (\overrightarrow{r}-\overrightarrow{r_q}) \over |\overrightarrow{r}-\overrightarrow{r_q}|^3 } $$

The position vector of the charge $\overrightarrow{r_p}$ can be represented by an arrow from the origin to the location of the charge. Let $\overrightarrow{r}$ be the position vector of a point in space. The vector $(\overrightarrow{r} - \overrightarrow{r_p})$ describes the diplacement vector of the point from the position of the charge.

The magnitude of the displacement vector, $|\overrightarrow{r} - \overrightarrow{r_p}|$ is large when there is greater spatial separation between the point and the charge. Therefore, the magnitude of the magnetic field at point $\overrightarrow{r}$ is smaller when the charge is farther away from the point.

In the below example, there is a positive charge moving along the +x-axis. For a point $\overrightarrow{r}$ on the y-z plane, the cross product of the velocity $\overrightarrow{v}$ and the displacement vector $(\overrightarrow{r} - \overrightarrow{r_p})$ is a vector that is perpendicular to both $(\overrightarrow{v})$ and $(\overrightarrow{r} - \overrightarrow{r_p})$.
